Program Offers

Department of Industrial Technology

Its flagship program is Bachelor of Science in Industrial Technology (BSIT) with the following majors: Mechanical Technology, Automotive Technology, Electrical Technology, Food Technology, Drafting and Garments. In 2012, the program passed the preliminary survey prior to a Level I accreditation. The program is intended for a professional Industrial Technologist career including a leading role in the br production, manufacturing and enhancement of technology management.

-

College of Criminal Justice Education

The program develops physically fit young men and women who
dedicate themselves to a career in law enforcement and public safety; to become competent,proficient, skilled, scientific, and technically well-trained vanguards of society equipped with knowledge and training in modern law enforcement work, scientific crime detection and investigation, corrections and social defense.

-

Department Computer Studies

The program Computer Studies offers three(3) specialty courses; Information Technology, Computer Engineering, and Computer Science. The program focuses technological advancement through education on computer mechanism and programming. It aims to produce highly competitive scientists that prove the quality of its education.

-

Department of General Teacher Training

The College of Teacher Education aims to create world class scholars in the teaching profession. It offers several specialty courses, such as: Primary and Secondary education majors in English, Filipino, Math, and Science.

The teaching profession is the foundation of future education and is thus taken seriously as a profession of hard work, difficulty, and rewards in the form of succesful lives of students.

-

Department of Business Administration

The Department of Business Administration offers courses majoring in Financial Management and Human Resource Management. It aims to widen the future prospects of our next generation marketeers and business entrepreneurs. The program focuses on enliving the market through the introduction of new innovations nurtured in the school grounds.


Our History

History

Surigao del Sur State University (SDSSU), formerly Surigao del Sur Polytechnic State College (SSPSC), is composed of seven (7) campuses stretching from north to south of the Province of Surigao del Sur with its northernmost campus located in the Municipality of Cantilan and its southernmost campus located in the City of Bislig, with the Main Campus at the center in the City of Tandag. It achieved its status as a university through Republic Act 9998 signed on February 22, 2010 by former President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo.

The history of the University dates back to 1982 when Bukidnon State University (BSU), then Bukidnon State College (BSC), opened its extension in Tandag City known as the Bukidnon External Studies Center (BESC). But this did not last long and after 10 years, the Surigaonuns witnessed the birth of Surigao del Sur Polytechnic College (SSPC) with the signing into law of Republic Act 7377 on April 10, 1992 by the late President Corazon C. Aquino.

The creation of the Polytechnic College amalgamated the BESC in Tandag City and four (4) DECS-supervised schools in the municipalities of Cagwait, Tagbina, Lianga, and San Miguel (formerly Tago) into one system. Six years later, SSPC earned the status of being a state college by virtue of Republic Act 8628 signed into law on April 23, 1998. This development also led Surigao del Sur Institute of Technology (SSIT) in the Municipality of Cantilan to join the other external campuses under the umbrella of Surigao del Sur Polytechnic State College (SSPSC) further spreading the newly-established state college’s influence and services into the northern part of the Province.

Finally, the efforts of pioneering officials, faculty and staff, administrators of the campuses, with the support of LGUs, district representatives of the Province and other stakeholders and the success of its programs eventually led to the conversion of the state-run college finally into a State University in 2010 by virtue of RA 9998.

Recently, a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) with the University of Southeastern Philippines (USEP) in Davao City allowed for a co-management of USEP Bislig Campus until 2022. This will allow a transition period before the latter’s full integration into the system making it the 7th campus of SDSSU.

This expansion policy of the University is showing no sign of slowing down with other public higher education schools or LUCs being worked out for possible integration into the system. But this only manifests the trust and confidence created and cemented by SDSSU with its stakeholders as partners over the years in its quest and pursuit for world-class quality tertiary education for the youth of Surigao del Sur and nearby provinces.

 


Mandate

The university shall primarily provide advanced education, higher technological, professional instruction and training in trade, fishery, agriculture, science, education, commerce, engineering, forestry, nautical courses and other related fields.

It shall also undertake research and extension services and provide progressive leadership in its areas of specialization (RA 9998, Section 2).

 


Vision and Mission

Vision

A transformative leading University in Asia and the Pacific.

Mission

NEMSU shall provide competency-based higher education training driven by relevant and responsive instruction, research, extension and sustainable resource management.

 


Core Values

Competence

A combination of observable and measurable knowledge, skills, abilities, and personal attributes that contribute to enhance the performance of NEMSU employees and ultimately result in organizational success.

Accountability

Responsibility for own actions and decisions, and commitment to accomplish work in an ethical, efficient, cost-effective and transparent manner manifesting the value of sound stewardship in the use of resources for common good.

Responsiveness

A prompt action and release of consistent quality communication that is focus on providing correct and complete action and/or information to clients and stakeholders.

Excellence

Txhe quality spectrum at exceptional levels demonstrated by learning outcomes and the development of shared culture of quality consistent with the vision, mission and goals of University.

Service

Dedication for a continual improvement of services, stakeholder’s relationships, and partnership which stresses interdependence and collaboration for a sustainable success of clients and their communities in helping build a just, peaceful, stable and progressive Filipino nation.


Goals

Instruction

To develop competitive, resilient and transformative academic community.

Research

To produce high impact research and development for the advancement of knowledge and instructional innovation, institutional development and policy formulation, and commercialization.

Extension

To improve the lives of the rural and urban poor through transfer of technology and knowledge.

Production

To develop and improve resource generation to sustain University operations.

Governance

To deliver transparent, responsive and excellent governance for client satisfaction.


NEMSU CARES...

These core values are not descriptions of the work we do, nor the strategies we employ to accomplish our University vision. They are the core values that underlie our works and interactions as we internalize responsibilities to fulfill our mission and vision. They are the basic elements of how we go about our work and how we deal with clients and stakeholders, how we mold students to become competent, innovative, globally competitive and service-oriented individuals.
